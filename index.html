Economics of Crime: Data Dashboards

By Matthias Olson

This dashboard examines fatal police pursuits in the U.S. (2017–2021), exploring how fatal incidents are distributed across time, geography, and race. The treemap and calendar heatmap highlight racial disparities and time-based patterns in pursuit-related deaths.

This global terrorism dashboard visualizes regional and temporal trends in terrorist activity (1970–2019), showing variation in attack types and regional prevalence using bubble and stacked area charts from the Global Terrorism Database.

This dashboard explores gun violence trends in the U.S. (2014–2017) using a hex map, calendar heatmap, and bump chart to highlight impact by state and patterns by day of the week and month.

This dashboard analyzes U.S. crime trends from 1960 to 2022, featuring visualizations of murder and property crime rates by region and state. It also highlights long-term crime rate patterns in South Carolina.

This visualization presents incarceration rates in South Carolina by county from 1970–2017. It includes prison and jail maps and a stacked area chart segmented by urbanicity, showing how incarceration rates have evolved over time.

This dashboard compares crime trends across major South Carolina universities, focusing on sexual misconduct, aggravated assault, and burglary per 100,000 students, normalized by university size.
