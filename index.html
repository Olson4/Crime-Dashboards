Fatal Police Pursuits Dashboard

This dashboard examines fatal police pursuits across the U.S. by race, location, and cause of pursuit.

In recent years, growing public attention has focused on the often-deadly consequences of police interactions, but one area that remains key is the fatality risk associated with police vehicle pursuits. These high-speed chases, sometimes initiated as a result of minor infractions, can often escalate quickly and end in fatality. Many of the fatalities associated with police vehicle pursuits aren’t just those being pursued, but also passengers, bystanders, and even police officers themselves. Furthermore, we see that Black and Latino individuals are disproportionately affected, raising urgent questions about how race, geography, and decision-making shape deadly outcomes. This project uses public data on fatal police pursuits from 2017 to 2021 to examine when these incidents happen, where they occur and who they affect. Through a combination of three visualizations built in Tableau, the dashboard aims to surface patterns that are often lost in raw statistics. Global Terrorism Dashboard

This global terrorism dashboard visualizes regional and temporal trends in terrorist activity (1970–2019), showing variation in attack types and regional prevalence using bubble and stacked area charts from the Global Terrorism Database.

Gun Violence Dashboard

Analyzes gun violence patterns by state, weekday, and season between 2014–2017.

Gun violence remains a deeply entrenched issue in the United States, affecting communities in a variety of different ways depending on factors such as when and where incidents occur. While national media tends to narrow in on mass shootings or raw death counts, these figures often overlook important nuances such as the role of population size, regional differences, or shifting trends over time. Incarceration Trends Dashboard

Visualizes jail and prison incarceration by county across South Carolina.

The incarceration rate in the U.S. declined from 2019 to the lowest rate since 1995, marking a substantial reversal after decades of growth in prison and jail populations. South Carolina’s incarceration trends closely mimic that of the national decline; however, significant disparities do exist at the county level.
